What Is Regenerative Tourism?


Regenerative tourism is a new form of travel that means leaving a place better than you found it. While volunteering abroad is a form of regenerative tourism, the concept is open to all types of travel. Regenerative tourism relies on caring hosts who are dedicated to working on projects that help improve their community and willing tourists who want to be a part of these projects and engage with the organizations, vendors and experts that provide these experiences.   • 20% Staking

    EMI tokens will be awarded to the highest contributing users on the platform every two weeks. Users that have contributed the most to the platform by volume will share an allotted amount of EMI tokens. There will be a fixed cap on how much EMI is awarded to each user per batch to ensure a wider distribution of tokens within the community.

  • 10% Platform Incentives

    Users will be able to earn bounties of EMI tokens by completing various activities on the platform such as profile completion, community engagement, user invites, completing volunteer trips and more.

  • 15% Team

    The Team allocation is reserved as compensation for current and future Emisari team members including advisors and partners. All Team tokens are subject to lockups and vesting periods starting one year after the initial token distribution.

  • 30% Treasury

    The Treasury fund will be allocated to fund all platform and ecosystem development activities such as feature developments, operation expenses, marketing bounties, cross-application integrations, and affiliate incentives. Treasury Fund tokens are subject to vesting periods beginning after initial token distribution.

  • 5% Liquidity

    The Liquidity Fund allocation is specifically reserved for fulfilling the needs of future platform integrations including providing liquidity and other bounties.

  • 10% Reserves

    The Reserve allocation will function as an emergency token pool and will not be released via any set schedule but only upon approval from EMI stakeholders.
